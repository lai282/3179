Spatial Distribution Pattern of Country
This map helps in understanding how popularity is distributed spatially across the world. We can reveal the following spatial distribution patterns by studying the relationship between popularity density and land area:
• Urbanization
  > Indicated by high population density in urban areas. Mostly characterized by dense populations and significant economic and industrial activity.
• Rural Areas
  > Low population density in vast land areas. Population are sparse and economic activities may be centered around agriculture and natural resource extraction.
• Regional Disparities
  > Variations in population density across different regions.

Findings:
• Monaco in Europe and Singapore in Asia are the most urbanized country as they have a very high population density considered their land area is small.
• Russia , Australia and Canada are the countries with a significant rural population due to its low population density and vast land area. These countries are among the top countries that has the most natural resources .
• Asia is the most populous continent in the world while Oceania has the lowest population densities in the world.

Role of Agricultural Sector
The following bar chart compares the GDP per capita of different countries and regions and assesses how much their land is dedicated to agricultural activites.

Findings:
• Among the countries with high agricultural land use, United Kingdom has the highest GDP per capita. This demonstrates that the country's ability to successfully balance agriculture with other sectors, resulting high standard of living and economic well-being for its people.
• Liechtenstein, having one the highest GDP per capita has only around 30% of its land dedicated to agriculture, indicating it has strong non-economic sectors contribute significantly to its economic success.
• Despite having significant rural population, Australia and Canada has successfully diversified their economies beyond agriculture , relying on natural resources and other industries to have high standard of living for their population.

Labor Market Dynamics
The following parallel coordinate plot will illustrates the labor market dynamics in various countries and explore the relationship between the labor force participation percentage, unemployment rate, life expectancy, and GDP per capita.
Findings:
• Countries with higher GDP per capita have high percentage of labor force participation population, low unemployment rate and high life expentancy. This can shows that countries with robust economies tend to contribute to better health outcomes for citizens and having healthy job market and job security.
• Countries with high unemployment rate exhibits lower GDP per capita. This shows how unemployment affects greatly the economic prosperity of the country.
• There are also countries with high percentage of labor force participation population and low employment rate having low GDP per capita. There may be other factors playing a significant role in shaping the economy of a country and further exploration is needed.
